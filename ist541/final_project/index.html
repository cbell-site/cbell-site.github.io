Point Lobos State Natural Reserve is a unique resource for students in California.
Here are some safety tips, rules, and guidelines for your visit.

Reserve Rule 1: Stay on the trails.

Visitors must stay on the trails.

This prevents erosion and damage to plants and small animals. It also keeps visitors safe from poison oak and other hazards like ticks. It keeps children away from cliff edges and rogue waves.

Some beaches can be visited, and others must be left alone for the animals.


Reserve Rule 2: Only eat in picnic areas.

Visitors can only eat in the picnic areas. This reduces litter and protects animals from eating human food.


Reserve Rule 3: No collecting.

Visitors cannot take any organism or natural object from Point Lobos. No leaves, no rocks, no shells, no feathers, etc.

Students can pick up interesting objects to look at them, but then must put the items back where they were found.


Courtesy: Limit noise and chatter.

Encourage your students to talk quietly to each other. It shows respect to other visitors, and makes it more likely that they will see animals along their route.

This is especially important during harbor seal pupping season, when new babies and their mothers are resting on the beaches of Point Lobos. Disturbing or scaring the animals could put them at risk.


Safety Tip: Avoid poison oak.

Poison oak is a native plant that grows all over the Reserve. The oils from its leaves or branches may cause an itchy rash.

Encourage kids to wear long pants or leggings for their visit to Point Lobos. Show them the plant before they begin the walk. It won't harm them if they stay on the trails. You should check nearby when inviting kids to touch other plants.
